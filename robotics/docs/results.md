## GEAR-SONIC WBC clone <a id="wbc"></a>

**Date:** April 2026.

**Goal:** have a low-level open-source Whole-Body Controller as a baseline to measure what GR00T actually adds.

**Result:** working clone, integrated on G1. Used as a reference point to measure GR00T's real contribution vs a classical controller. Partial conclusion: GR00T excels at **generalizing to novel configurations** (e.g. unknown sloped terrain) — a classical WBC remains competitive on strictly trained tasks.

## Hy3D → Isaac Sim pipeline <a id="hy3d"></a>

**Tool:** Tencent Hunyuan3D (Hy3D).

**Workflow:**
1. Text or image prompt → GLB mesh generated by Hy3D.
2. SCP the GLB to `/home/panda/robotics/assets/custom/` on Spark.
3. `add_reference_to_stage()` in the Isaac Sim launch script.
4. CollisionAPI applied where needed (static = convex hull).

## Go2 patrol + VLM (E2E) <a id="patrol"></a>

**Scripts:** `patrol_circle.py`, `patrol_square.py`, `patrol_demo.py`, `patrol_mission1.py`, `patrol_mission2.py`, `patrol_mission3.py`.

**Full pipeline validated:**

```
Go2 camera (640x480)
    ↓ JPEG @ 2.5 Hz
mono_robot_look.py
    ↓
GPT-4o VLM (vision)
    ↓ text description
Negation-filter regex
    ↓ animal/human classification
Agentic decision
    ↓ if human:
    "IDENTIFY YOURSELF" → simulated reply
    ↓
GPT-4o decision (vs authorized_personnel.json)
```

**Metric: false positives.** Without the negation filter, the VLM produced ~80% false positives ("no people" matched as "people"). After regex filter on 4 negation patterns (FR/EN): **100% of false positives eliminated** across 16 test scans.

**Metric: E2E.** Mission 3 (fox on south wall + Doctor David on east wall) — the robot correctly discriminates animal vs human, identifies, and triggers the right alarm. Validated in simulation.

## PID yaw correction <a id="pid"></a>

**Problem:** the `Velocity-Rough-Unitree-Go2` policy drifts about 80° in 30 seconds during straight walk (`vx=0.3, vy=0, vz=0`). Known limitation of the pre-trained model.

**Solution:** software PID layered on top of the policy.

```python
YAW_KP = 0.03
YAW_KI = 0.003
YAW_KD = 0.003
YAW_WZ_MAX = 0.15
YAW_CORRECTION_HZ = 4
```

The robot reads its actual yaw at 4 Hz, computes the error against the desired heading, injects a bounded corrective wz. The RL policy receives a non-zero wz which keeps it aligned (and avoids the "pure rotation" crash that appears with `vx=0`).

**Measured result:**

| Metric | Without PID | With PID v2 |
|---|---|---|
| 30s lateral drift | 5–8 m | **0.55 m** |
| Drift/forward ratio | 60–80% | **6.2%** |
| Stable yaw error | 80°+ | **±1°** |

Validated over 9 m travelled at 0.3 m/s, on the Isaac Sim ground plane.

## walk-these-ways / unitree_rl_gym compatibility

**Verdict:** not directly compatible with Isaac Sim 5.1 / Isaac Lab 2.3.

| Repo | Sim | Obs dims | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Teddy-Liao/walk-these-ways-go2` | Isaac Gym Preview 4 (deprecated) | 70 + 2100 history | NOT drop-in |
| `Improbable-AI/walk-these-ways` (MIT) | Isaac Gym Preview 4 | 42 (Go1 only) | Not applicable |
| `unitreerobotics/unitree_rl_gym` | Isaac Gym Preview 4 | 48 | NOT drop-in |
| **Isaac Lab 2.3 (our setup)** | Isaac Sim 5.1 | 235 (height scan) | — |

To port a policy from any of these into Isaac Lab: rebuild the observation vector respecting order and scaling, provide the history buffer for adaptation-module architectures. Realistic but non-trivial work.

**Practical decision:** stay on the native Isaac Lab rough_model + PID correction; fine-tune later if a specific need emerges.
